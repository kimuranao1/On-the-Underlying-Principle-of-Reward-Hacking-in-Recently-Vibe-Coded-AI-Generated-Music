Statistical Analysis of Relative Phase Melody Structure

This report summarizes the results of a statistical analysis of MIDI data from multiple classical works, extracting melodies as their relative phase motion structure rather than pitch.

This analysis is not based on music theory, but rather on mathematical structural analysis.

Analysis Method

  • Converting Simultaneous Notes to Single Notes (Extracting Highest Note)
  • Analysis by Track
  • Automatic Pitch Estimation
  • Tonic Normalization
  • Pitch Difference Extraction
  • Triple Structure Extraction
  • Relative Phase Normalization

This final step allows comparison to be made not only on the pitch values ​​themselves, but also on the movement shapes.

Most Important Observation

Melody is not something that "moves," but something that "returns."

The most common structures across all data were (0,1,0) and (0,-1,0).

Mathematically speaking, this is 

Deviation → Restoration

This indicates that the fundamental motion of melody is oscillatory motion.
